Assumptions & caveats
  • Polar body = animal pole. The extruded polar body is the accepted landmark of the animal pole; the opposite side is vegetal. (We do not distinguish 1st vs 2nd polar body — both sit at the same animal-pole site.)
  • Sperm = GFP midpiece. The manually-labelled midpiece is a proxy for the sperm entry position: it stays near the fusion site while the sperm head decondenses into the pronucleus and migrates centrally.
  • First cleavage plane is read directly in two-cell embryos as the interface between the two blastomeres; in zygotes we use the pronuclear axis as the predicted normal (Hiiragi's proposed determinant).
  • Shape control. Because the cleavage plane can simply follow embryo elongation, we also report every angle against the embryo's own long axis; if the signal tracks shape, it is not independent pre-patterning.
  • Modest n and pre-specified test. 27 zygotes, 19 two-cell (fewer with a segmented polar body); the hypotheses are fixed in advance and compared to an exact random-orientation null.
